# Learning Methodology Guide

## Scientific Learning Methods

This app is built on four proven learning methods:

### 1. Spaced Repetition (SM-2 Algorithm)

**What is it?**
A technique that schedules reviews at increasing intervals to maximize long-term retention.

**How it works:**
- First review: 1 day later
- Second review: 3 days later
- Third review: 7 days later
- Fourth review: 14 days later
- Continues with increasing intervals based on your performance

**Why it works:**
Reviews material just before you're about to forget it, strengthening memory pathways.

**In this app:**
- Words are automatically scheduled based on the SM-2 algorithm
- "Remembered" increases the interval
- "Forgot" resets to day 1
- After 3+ successful reviews, words are marked "Mastered"

### 2. Active Recall

**What is it?**
Testing yourself to retrieve information from memory rather than passive re-reading.

**How it works:**
- See a prompt (example sentence)
- Try to recall the meaning before seeing the answer
- Test your memory actively

**Why it works:**
The effort to retrieve information strengthens neural connections better than passive review.

**In this app:**
- Flashcards show the example sentence first
- You must try to recall before flipping
- Tests force active retrieval
- No passive vocabulary lists in study mode

### 3. Context-Based Learning

**What is it?**
Learning words within meaningful sentences and real-world usage rather than in isolation.

**How it works:**
- Every word includes an example sentence
- Common phrases show real usage
- Words are never shown alone

**Why it works:**
Context provides memory anchors and shows practical usage, making words more memorable and usable.

**In this app:**
- Every word has a required example sentence
- Common chunks (phrases) are included
- Flashcards prioritize context over isolated words
- Example sentences are shown first

### 4. Chunking

**What is it?**
Grouping information into meaningful units that are easier to remember.

**How it works:**
- Learn phrases, not just individual words
- Group related concepts together
- Create mental "chunks" of information

**Why it works:**
The brain processes chunks as single units, increasing memory capacity and recall speed.

**In this app:**
- Each word includes common phrases (chunks)
- Words are learned with their typical combinations
- HSK levels group similar difficulty words

## Optimal Study Routine

### Daily Practice (10-15 minutes)

**Morning (5 minutes)**
- Check dashboard for words due today
- Review all due flashcards
- Mark honestly: "Remembered" or "Forgot"

**Evening (5-10 minutes)**
- Add 10-15 new words from vocabulary library
- Take a mini test (5-10 questions)
- Review bookmarked difficult words

### Weekly Goals

- **Consistency**: Study every day to maintain streak
- **New Words**: 70-100 new words per week
- **Review**: All due cards reviewed same day
- **Testing**: 2-3 tests per week
- **Accuracy**: Aim for 80%+ on tests

### Monthly Progress

- **Check Stats**: Review your progress monthly
- **Adjust Pace**: Increase/decrease new words based on accuracy
- **Celebrate**: Acknowledge mastered words milestone

## Tips for Success

### 1. Focus on Understanding, Not Speed
- Read example sentences carefully
- Understand context before memorizing
- Quality over quantity

### 2. Use Pinyin Strategically
- Show Pinyin when learning new words
- Hide Pinyin to test character recognition
- Toggle as needed for your level

### 3. Listen to Pronunciation
- Use text-to-speech feature
- Repeat out loud
- Train your ear while learning

### 4. Be Honest with Ratings
- Don't mark "Remembered" if you hesitated
- Better to review more than to fake progress
- The algorithm adjusts to your honesty

### 5. Maintain Your Streak
- Even 5 minutes counts
- Consistency beats intensity
- Build the habit first

### 6. Use Tests Regularly
- Tests reveal what you actually know
- Different from flashcard review
- Provides valuable feedback

### 7. Bookmark Difficult Words
- Save challenging words
- Review bookmarks separately
- Extra practice on weak points

## Learning Phases

### Phase 1: New (Just Added)
- Word added to your queue
- First review scheduled immediately
- Focus: Understanding context and meaning

### Phase 2: Learning (Active Study)
- Words being reviewed repeatedly
- Intervals increasing gradually
- Focus: Active recall and repetition

### Phase 3: Mastered (3+ Reviews)
- Successfully reviewed 3+ times
- Longer intervals between reviews
- Focus: Maintenance and retention

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Learning Too Many Words at Once
Add 10-15 words daily, not 100 at once

### ❌ Skipping Reviews
Due words pile up and become overwhelming

### ❌ Marking "Remembered" Too Easily
Be honest to let the algorithm work properly

### ❌ Ignoring Context
Don't just memorize the word, understand its usage

### ❌ Passive Review
Actively try to recall before revealing answers

### ❌ Inconsistent Study
Daily 10 minutes beats weekly 1 hour

## Measuring Progress

### Key Metrics

**Words Learned**
Total words in your study queue

**Words Mastered**
Words with 3+ successful reviews

**Accuracy Rate**
Percentage of correct answers in tests

**Current Streak**
Consecutive days of study

**Total XP**
Overall learning effort

### What Good Progress Looks Like

- **Week 1**: 50-70 words learned, 60%+ accuracy
- **Week 2**: 100-150 words learned, 70%+ accuracy
- **Month 1**: 300-400 words learned, 50+ mastered, 80%+ accuracy
- **Month 3**: 800-1000 words learned, 200+ mastered, 85%+ accuracy

## Adjustment Strategies

### If Accuracy < 70%
- Reduce new words to 5-10 per day
- Spend more time on context
- Review bookmarked words more

### If Reviews Pile Up
- Catch up before adding new words
- Dedicate one day to clearing backlog
- Reduce daily new words temporarily

### If Losing Streak
- Set calendar reminder
- Study at same time daily
- Start with just 5 minutes

## Remember

Learning a language is a marathon, not a sprint. This app uses science to make your learning efficient, but consistency is key. Study a little every day, be honest with yourself, and trust the process.
